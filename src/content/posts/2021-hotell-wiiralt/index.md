## proposal

the proposal addresses how hotels can isolate locals and create deficits
in public space. the design aims to establish a welcoming and cultural
environment for all and to encompass both local and touristic interests
through honest scale, natural courtyards, revitalisation, lighting, and
multi-age programming.

key features —

- a gallery commemorating a demolished 100-year-old EKA building
- facade materiality reflecting existing buildings, softened by greenery
- multi-form structure with stepped sections preventing neighbour shadowing
- courtyard with decked seating and vegetation
- a public sauna accessible to all ages
- a café with grab-and-go and lounge areas
- transparent circulation creating bright, engaging spaces
