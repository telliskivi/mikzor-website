<span class="red">tehnoülevaatus</span> / <span class="red">vehicle inspection</span>.

2023/24 winter semester project. an architectural study of common
rituals and their associated semiotics.

studio: <span class="blue">Sam Jacob Studio at Angewandte</span>.
site: <span class="blue">the neolithic cromlech of Vale Maria do Meio, Portugal</span>.
collaborators: <span class="blue">John Clayson, Jonas Ramoser, Erfan Khosravi</span>.

## concept

the design interprets the vehicle-inspection (MOT) ritual through
neolithic stone-circle symbolism, creating circular circulation with an
east–west orientation.

key elements —

- a *yellow and black lines — you may stop but not park* floor pattern
- a palladian-grid plan in the testing chapel
- two remaining stones from the cromlech form the entrance facade

the project functions as an <span class="red">automotive swan song</span>
amid urban pedestrianisation trends.
